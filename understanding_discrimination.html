Understanding Discrimination

What is Discrimination?

Discrimination is the unfair or prejudicial treatment of people and groups based on characteristic such as race, gender, age, or sexual orientation, (American Psychological Association). Discrimination is a world-wide issue that we always need to be working towards reducing, in order to build a better and more sustainable future for not just us in the United States, but all people worldwide. In order to reduce discrimination and its consequences, we must first know what causes it and what impacts it exactly has.


What Causes Discrimination?

  • Ignorance/Lack of Education
  • Fear and Misinformation
  • Cultural/Societal Norms
  • Legal/Systemic Laws and Policies
  • Stereotypes/Biases
  • Media/Representation
  • Group Identity/Tribalism
  • Economic inequality
  • Politics
  • And more...

How Does Discrimination Impact Society?

  • People Getting Less Opportunities
  • Mental Distress And/Or Disorders
  • Physical Harm And/Or Death
  • Societal Division (Segregation)
  • Mistrust & Societal Conflict
  • Less Education
  • Economic Struggles
  • Human Rights Violations
  • Hindering Democratic Participation
  • Promoting Discriminatory Laws/Policies
