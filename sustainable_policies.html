Sustainable Policies for Equality

What are Sustainable Policies?

The United Nations’ Sustainable Development Goals are a set of sustainable goals set by the UN in order to achieve “peace and prosperity for the people and the planet” by the year 2030. One of the main components of these goals is to decrease discrimination and promote equality. The UN is not the only organization to set goals like this however, other organizations like our government, the world’s governments, and other non-government organizations (NGOs) have sustainable policies in place in order to promote a better world. Generally, there are 10 main policies/policy topics you will notice among the various sustainable goals worldwide.


1. Inclusive Economic Growth:

Ensuring economic opportunities are available to all people, including marginalized communities, allows people and communities to start supporting themselves and prevents discrimination by allowing all those who wish to participate in an economy to do so, (as in have the jobs, businesses, and careers they wish to work in).

2. Access to Education and Skills Development:

Having proper access to education not only empowers individuals and allows them to pursue their passions, but it also bolsters world economic growth as a whole for a given area. Education is also very important to new discoveries, technological advancements, and the betterment of the world overall. It also serves as determent to discrimination as one of the causes of discrimination is ignorance/biases.

3. Universal Healthcare:

There’s a saying, ‘health is wealth’. Adequate and affordable access to healthcare for all individuals regardless of socioeconomic status, ethnicity, nationality, or etc., makes it so that there’s less discrimination and more equality/fair treatment in healthcare. It also allows individuals to be self-sufficient and pursue their goals.

4. Gender Equality:

Gender discrimination, otherwise known as sexism, is one of the most common forms of discrimination in the world today. It is important to have policies that have specific measures in place in order to prevent gender discrimination and instead promote gender equality ensuring that equal opportunities and treatment are present in the workplace, educational sectors, healthcare, and etc.

5. Social Protection Systems:

Social protection systems are porgram such as unemployement benefits (for people don't or can't work), pensions (for people who retired/are too old to work), disability benefits (for people who are phsyically or mentally unable to work), afforable housing (for people who can't afford housing costs), and more that make sure people can meet their basic needs even when times are rough.

6. Environmental Justice:

Addressing and taking action on the effects of climate change and other environmental threats such as wildfires, deforestation, oil and gas development, etc., to protect our environment and the world we live in. This also includes maintaining and protecting native/indigenous reserves from being exploited by corporate corporations.

7. Anti-Discrimination Laws and Equal Rights Protections:

Enforcing laws that protect individuals from discrimination on the basis of race, gender, ethnicity, nationality, disability, etc., and also ensuring that individuals that violate those laws are prosecuted accordingly. This is essential in order to make sure that these laws are actually making a difference in people’s lives.

8. Participatory Governance:

Making sure that all communities have the tools and resources they need to have a voice in decision-making processes and that they are not barred from having a say in the government, especially those who have historically been excluded from having those privileges.

9. Affordable Housing and Urban Development:

Having access to housing is a basic human right and is essential in today’s world in order to have any sort of employment. However, many low-income individuals/communities do not have the resources to afford housing. So affordable housing and urban development (building new houses) helps empower these communities and allows them to have a place to call home.

10. Redistribution of Wealth:

Most of the wealth in the world is held by the top 5% of the world. Part of this is due to taxation systems that give breaks to big corporations who have millions, billions, or even trillions of dollars while not affording that same treatment middle class and low-income earners. Policies that would tax big companies more and use that money to contirbute to society in a meaningful way would help bring more opportunities to the people who need it.
